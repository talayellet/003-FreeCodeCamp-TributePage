J.K. Rowling

The novelist who wrote the Harry Potter fantasy series

Joanne Rowling, born 31 July 1965, who writes under the pen names J. K. Rowling and Robert Galbraith, is a British novelist and screenwriter who wrote the Harry Potter fantasy series.

Here's a time line of J. K. Rowling's life:

  • 1965 - Born in Yate, Gloucestershire, England
  • 1982 - Rowling took the entrance exams for Oxford University but was not accepted and read for a B.A. in French and Classics at the University of Exeter
  • 1986 - Rowling graduated from Exeter and moved to London to work as a researcher and bilingual secretary for Amnesty International
  • 1988 - Rowling wrote a short essay about her time studying Classics titled "What was the Name of that Nymph Again?
  • 1990 - While Rowling was on a four-hour-delayed train trip from Manchester to London, the idea for a story of a young boy attending a school of wizardry "came fully formed" into her mind
  • 1992 - On 16 October Rowling got married to a Portuguese television journalist named Jorge Arantes
  • 1993 - 27 July Rowling gave birth to their first child, Jessica Isabel Rowling Arantes in Portugal
  • 1993 - The couple separated on 17 November
  • 1993 - In December, Rowling and her then-infant daughter moved to Edinburgh, Scotland, to be near Rowling's sister
  • 1995 - Rowling finished her manuscript for Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone on an old manual typewriter
  • 1997 - Bloomsbury published Philosopher's Stone with an initial print run of 1,000 copies, 500 of which were distributed to libraries
  • 1999 - Its sequel, Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets, was published in July 1998 and again Rowling won the Smarties Prize
  • 2000 - The fourth book, Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire, was released simultaneously in the UK and the US on 8 July 2000 and broke sales records in both countries
  • 2005 - The sixth book, Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince, was released on 16 July. It too broke all sales records, selling nine million copies in its first 24 hours of release
  • 2007 - The title of the seventh and final Harry Potter book was announced as Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows
